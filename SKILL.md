# AI Business Network Podcast Docket Writer

## Overview

Create broadcast-ready dockets for the AI Business Network podcast—a show covering AI developments through a business and go-to-market lens. Episodes run 30-40 minutes with 3-4 segments.

**Target audience**: Founders, business executives, GTM operators (NOT engineers/developers)

## Docket Creation Workflow

### Step 1: Gather & Assess Input

Collect all topics, links, and raw information provided. For each item:
- Identify the core topic/news
- Note any provided sources or links
- Flag gaps requiring research

### Step 2: Research Enhancement

For each topic, use web search to:
1. Find the original source (company blog, press release, research paper)
2. Gather 2-3 additional perspectives or analyses
3. Find relevant statistics or data points
4. Identify business impact angles not obvious from headlines
5. Look for contrarian takes or critiques

**Research priorities**:
- Revenue/funding implications
- Market size and growth data
- Competitive landscape shifts
- Real-world adoption examples
- Expert opinions from business leaders (not just technologists)

### Step 3: Topic Selection & Ordering

From all gathered material, select 3-4 topics based on:

**Selection criteria** (in priority order):
1. **Business impact** - Direct implications for revenue, operations, or strategy
2. **Actionability** - Listeners can do something with this information
3. **Timeliness** - Relevant to current moment, not stale news
4. **Audience relevance** - Matters to founders/executives/GTM operators
5. **Discussion potential** - Generates interesting debate or multiple angles

**Ordering logic**:
- **Segment 1**: Lead with the most impactful/timely story (8-10 min)
- **Segment 2**: Strong second topic, different category than first (8-10 min)
- **Segment 3**: Practical/tactical topic or emerging trend (8-10 min)
- **Segment 4** (optional): Quick hits, predictions, or lighter topic (5-7 min)

### Step 4: Build Each Segment

For each segment, create:

1. **Headline** - Punchy, broadcast-ready title
2. **The Story** - 2-3 sentence summary of what happened
3. **Why It Matters** - Business implications for the audience
4. **Key Data Points** - 3-5 stats, figures, or facts to reference
5. **Discussion Angles** - 2-3 questions or provocations for hosts
6. **Contrarian/Alternative View** - What skeptics or critics say
7. **Actionable Takeaway** - What listeners should consider doing
8. **Sources** - Links to primary and secondary sources

### Step 5: Add Show Elements

Include:
- **Cold open hook** - Attention-grabbing opening line or question
- **Transitions** - Suggested segues between topics
- **Timestamps** - Estimated timing for each segment
- **Callback opportunities** - Connections to previous episodes if relevant

## Topic Categories

Balance episodes across these categories:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **AI Product Launches** | New tools, features, platform updates |
| **Funding & M&A** | Investments, acquisitions, valuations |
| **Enterprise Adoption** | Case studies, implementation stories |
| **GTM & Sales AI** | Revenue tools, sales automation, marketing AI |
| **Regulatory & Policy** | Legislation, guidelines, compliance |
| **Workforce Impact** | Jobs, skills, organizational changes |
| **AI Strategy** | Frameworks, best practices, methodologies |
| **Emerging Tech** | New capabilities, research breakthroughs |

## Business Angle Translation

Always translate technical news into business terms:

| Technical Framing | Business Framing |
|-------------------|------------------|
| "Model achieves SOTA on benchmark" | "What this means for accuracy/reliability in production" |
| "New architecture reduces compute" | "Cost implications for AI deployment at scale" |
| "Open source release" | "Build vs. buy calculus just changed" |
| "API price reduction" | "Unit economics for AI-powered products" |
| "Multimodal capabilities" | "New use cases now viable for your business" |

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing docket, verify:

- [ ] Each segment has clear business relevance stated
- [ ] No segment relies solely on technical details
- [ ] At least one contrarian view included per segment
- [ ] All sources are linked and accessible
- [ ] Timing adds up to 30-40 minutes total
- [ ] Topics span at least 2 different categories
- [ ] Actionable takeaways are specific, not generic
- [ ] Data points are sourced and recent (within 30 days preferred)
